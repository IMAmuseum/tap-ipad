Talk
IMA Curator of Contemporary Art Sarah Urist Green @ Butler University
Thursday, April 18 / 7 pm
Butler University
Free
One of China’s most provocative and vocal artists, Ai Weiwei’s focus on human rights and social change eventually led to his nearly three-month detainment by Chinese authorities and ongoing surveillance, which have influenced his work enormously. Curator of contemporary art Sarah Urist Green discusses the IMA’s exhibition, which includes examples from the broad spectrum of the artist’s practice—ranging from sculpture, photography, and video to site-specific architectural installations.

May 16, 17 & 18
Film
Ai Weiwei: Never Sorry
(2012, dir. Alison Klayman, 91 mins., R)
Thursday, May 16 / 6:30 pm
Friday, May 17 / 6:30 pm (Join us after the film for Omnimic: Speak Your Art Out)
Saturday, May 18 / 2 pm
The Toby
Public Ticket $9, Member Ticket $5*
Between 2008 and 2011, director Alison Klayman was given unprecedented access to artist Ai Weiwei—beginning with his work on the “Bird’s Nest” Olympic stadium to the aftermath of his government detention. The resulting film is a touching, inspirational, and sometimes humorous portrait of a man whose roles as artist and activist cannot be separated.

June 8, 15, 22 & 29
Class
Connecting the Past to the Present: Telling Stories with Photographs
4 Saturdays, June 8, 15, 22 & 29 / 10 am–4 pm
Adult Lecture Study A
Public Ticket $240, Member Ticket $200*
Chinese artist Ai Weiwei uses photography as a way to document his personal life and the changing nature of urban landscapes. After visiting the exhibition, participants will work with a teaching artist who will introduce basic concepts of photography and discuss cameras, storytelling, and photo editing techniques. Each of the 4 sessions will combine theory and practice by encouraging participants to create a series of photographs taken both onsite at the IMA and in downtown Indianapolis. Participants are invited to bring their own point-and-shoot or digital SLR cameras. The IMA will provide the rest.

June 27
Film
Ai Weiwei's Fairytale
(2007, dir. Ai Weiwei, 152 mins., NR)
Thursday, June 27 / 7 pm
The Toby
Public Ticket $9, Member Ticket $5
This film documents a project produced for the European art event, Documenta 12, in which Ai invited 1,001 Chinese citizens of various ages and backgrounds to come to Kassel, Germany. They were asked to examine their dreams for the future, as well as the artist's own. Subtitled in English, the film examines the process beginning with the preparations for the project, through the challenges that the participants faced, as well as the artist's ideas behind the work.

Come early and see the exhibition Ai Weiwei: According to What?, and receive $1 off film admission at the IMA box office by showing your exhibition ticket stub.
